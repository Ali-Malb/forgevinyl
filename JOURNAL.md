I'm creating a magnetic interactive "turntable" station for my fridge. Put a custom mini 3D printed/acrylic vinyl record (representing a favourite album) on the station, it reads a custom embedded NFC/RFID tag, flashes a custom coloured LED sequence mirroring the album's aesthetics and automatically plays the top track from its built-in MP3 module and speaker. I just finished designing  the pcb and I'm excited to make tha casing. time: 3 hours

I won't lie, it took way longer than I would have liked to complete the case design due to the fact that fitting everything into a 24mm box that wasn't meant to be modified was a headache. I spent hours and hours trying to get the side-wall speaker grille slots lined up just right. Then, I spent a lot of time figuring out how to make four very exact screw holes to attach the speaker and found those plastic screw pillars would take up too much space and would probably make the case rattle anyway. I had to abandon that part and redo it for a very simple tape-on/ tiny alignment rails setup. It was hard to get the exact windows for the USB-C port and MicroSD slot right, to measure the back magnet pockets without ruining the front RFID paper window, but it's done and ready to be 3D printed!

time: 4 hours
